  • CSE 1146 - Intermediate Programing Spring '20, '21, '22
  • CSE 2037 - Introduction to Systems Programming Fall '22
  • EE 3072 - Dynamic Systems and Control Spring '21, '22, '23, '24
  • EE 2002 - Electrical Circuits II Spring '21
  • EE 2021 - Computer Tools for Electrical Engineering Fall '22
  • EE 1004 - Object-Oriented Programming Spring '22, '23, '24
  • EE 2003 - Digital Design ▼ Fall '25

    Instructor-led sessions covering number systems, Boolean algebra, logic simplification, and combinational and sequential circuit design. Weekly laboratory sessions using simulation tools (e.g., Logisim and Vivado) and hardware boards (e.g., FPGAs). Students design, simulate, and implement digital circuits to reinforce theoretical concepts. Design projects (individual or team-based) involving real-world digital systems (e.g., traffic light controller, vending machine, digital clock). Quizs and in-class problem-solving to assess understanding of weekly topics. A combination of theoretical exams and practical assessments (laboratory performance or design projects) in midterm and final assessments.
