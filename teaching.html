  • EE 3072 - Dynamic Systems and Control ▼

    Course Content: This course covers the fundamental modeling, analysis, and design of control systems. Key topics include open and closed-loop system modeling, time and frequency response analysis using PID, Bode, and Nyquist methods, and stability assessment via Routh-Hurwitz and Root Locus criteria. The curriculum also explores advanced state-space representations and digital controller design, with MATLAB applications integrated throughout.

    Taught Semesters: Spring '21, '22, '23, '24

  • EE 2002 - Electrical Circuits II ▼

    Course Content: Circuit analysis in s-domain. Circuit analysis in jw-domain.Impedance and admittance. Resonance circuits. Two-port networks: Z-parameters. T-equivalent of reciprocal network. Y-parameters. PI-equivalent of reciprocal networks. Conversion between Z and Y parameters. h-parameters. g-parameters. Transmission parameters. Initial value, steady and transient response of RLC circuits. Passive circuit synthesis: Positive real functions. Synthesis of 1-port LC, RC, RL, RLC networks. Implementation of Cauer I, Cauer II, Foster I and Foster II circuits. Active and passive filter applications: Passive filters; low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, band rejection filters. Active filters.

    Taught Semesters: Spring '21

  • EE 2021 - Computer Tools for Electrical Engineering ▼

    Course Content: Introduction to technical drawings in engineering design using computer technology. Introduction to a technical computing language and Graphical User Interface (GUI) design. Presenting simulation and design tools for circuits

    Taught Semesters:Fall '22

  • EE 1004 - Object-Oriented Programming ▼

    Course Content: This course provides a comprehensive foundation in programming using Java, progressing from basic algorithmic logic to advanced object-oriented programming principles (inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction) and essential data structures. Students will also learn modern software development techniques, including exception handling and file I/O, to design robust and reusable applications.

    Taught Semesters:Spring '22, '23, '24

  • EE 2003 - Digital Design ▼

    Course Content: Instructor-led sessions covering number systems, Boolean algebra, logic simplification, and combinational and sequential circuit design. Weekly laboratory sessions using simulation tools (e.g., Logisim and Vivado) and hardware boards (e.g., FPGAs). Students design, simulate, and implement digital circuits to reinforce theoretical concepts. Design projects (individual or team-based) involving real-world digital systems (e.g., traffic light controller, vending machine, digital clock). Quizs and in-class problem-solving to assess understanding of weekly topics. A combination of theoretical exams and practical assessments (laboratory performance or design projects) in midterm and final assessments.

    Taught Semesters:Fall '25

  • EE 3061 - Signals and Systems ▼

    Course Content: Classification of Signals and Basic Signal Properties. Some MATLAB Examples. Time Domain Models of Linear Time Invariant (LTI) Systems: Continuous time systems. Basic system properties. Causal LTI systems described by differential equations. System block diagrams. The solutions of differential equations. The unit impulse response and convolution integral. State variable analysis of LTI systems. Discrete time systems. Difference equations. The unit sample response and discrete convolution. Frequency Domain Models of LTI Sytems: Fourier series representation of continuous-time and discrete- time periodic signals. The continuous-time and discrete-time Fourier transform. Discrete Fourier transform. Time and frequency characterization of signals and systems. Frequency-domain analysis of systems. z-Transform and z-Domain Models of LTI Systems. Inverse z-transform. Impulse response and convolution. z-domain analysis of LTI differential systems. Pole-zero plot. Stability analysis.

    Taught Semesters:Fall '25
